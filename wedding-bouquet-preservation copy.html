A note on your flowers.

Because your heirloom is made from your actual blooms, colours will naturally soften during pressing and continue to fade gently over time.

This is part of working with a living medium, not a flaw. Each piece settles into softer, more muted tones as it ages.

UV-protective glass helps slow light exposure, though gradual change is natural and expected.

How your flowers are cared for

1. CHOOSE & BOOK

Choose your frame size and secure your wedding date. We take on a limited number of bookings each month, so booking early is recommended.

2. PLAN YOUR DROP-OFF

You will receive an email with everything you need, including bouquet care and a link to book your drop-off time. We recommend bringing your flowers within 3 days of your wedding, ideally the next day, while they are still fresh.

3. DROP OFF YOUR FLOWERS

Bring your bouquet to the studio in Osborne Park at your selected time. If you're away after your wedding, a loved one is welcome to bring your flowers on your behalf.

4. PRESS AND DESIGN

Your flowers are carefully pressed and dried over 8 weeks. Once ready, I create a layout proposal for you to review before your piece is brought to life.

5. FRAME & COLLECT

Your heirloom is professionally framed and made ready for collection. During peak season, the full process can take up to 6 months, and you’ll be updated at key stages along the way.

Questions

Everything you need to know

Still unsure? You’re always welcome to reach out before your booking. I’d love to hear about your wedding and help you choose the right package.

When should I book?

As soon as possible. I only accept 4 bookings per month and they fill quickly during wedding season.

You can book now and lock in your 2026-2027 date.

How long does the whole process take?

Pressing and creating your heirloom takes 8 to 12 weeks from the time your flowers arrive.

Please allow up to 5 months from your booking date. During peak wedding season, this can extend to 6 months, and I will keep you updated every step of the way.

What if my wedding is months away?

Book now and secure your spot. You don't need to do anything until after your wedding. I'm currently taking bookings for 2026 till June 2027.

Can you collect my bouquet?

Bouquet pick-up is available for an additional charges. Please email me to arrange the details.

I've already had my wedding. Can you still help?

Possibly, yes. Send me a photo of your flowers. If they've been air-dried and still hold some colour, I may be able to create something beautiful for you.

Reach out and we'll discuss your options.

Can I give this as wedding gift?

Absolutely. A bouquet preservation makes one of the most meaningful and lasting wedding gifts imaginable.

You can purchase a specific package from the shop, or contact me for a gift card option.

Do you work outside of Western Australia?

Due to flora and fauna safety regulations, I can only receive bouquets from within Western Australia.

How soon after my wedding do I need to drop off the flowers?

Within 1 to 3 days of your wedding. The fresher the flowers, the more vivid and beautiful the result. If you're heading straight to your honeymoon, a loved one can drop them off on your behalf. Drop-off is in Osborne Park.
